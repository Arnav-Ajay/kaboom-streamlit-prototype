# Kaboom Rules

## Objective

Win Kaboom by either:

1. finishing the game with the lowest total score
2. getting rid of all cards in your hand, which wins instantly

## Setup

- Default hand size is 4 cards per player.
- Maximum player count is 6. Four players is the recommended table size.
- All cards are dealt face down in front of each player.
- Cards stay separate in fixed positions. They are not stacked into one pile.

## Opening Peek

Before round 1 begins:

- each player may look at any 2 of their own cards
- those cards are returned face down
- this happens once only

## Card Values

- Ace = 1 point
- 2 through 10 = face value
- Jack = 10 points
- Queen = 10 points
- King = 10 points
- Red king = 0 points

Red king means king of hearts or king of diamonds.

## Turn Flow

On your turn:

1. draw 1 card from the deck
2. choose exactly one of these:
   - discard the drawn card
   - replace one card in your hand with the drawn card
   - if the drawn card has a power, discard it and use that power

If a power card ever goes into a hand, it loses its power and counts only for scoring.

## Powers

### 7 or 8

See one of your own cards.

- any suit
- choose exactly 1 of your own cards
- look once, then return it face down

### 9 or 10

See one card from another player.

- any suit
- choose exactly 1 card
- target must be another player
- look once, then return it face down

### Jack or Queen

Blind swap.

- any suit
- swap 1 of your own cards with 1 card from another player
- no peeking during the swap

### Black King

See and swap.

- king of spades or king of clubs only
- see both cards involved in the swap
- once seen, the swap is mandatory

## How Powers Work

Powers only come from the card you just drew.

To use a power:

1. draw a valid power card
2. discard that drawn card to the discard pile
3. resolve the power from that discard

Using the power is optional. You may discard the power card without using its power.

## Reactions

Whenever a card enters the discard pile, a reaction window opens for that rank.

This includes:

- normal discard
- replace, where the replaced hand card goes to discard
- power use, because the drawn power card is discarded first

### Matching Rule

If you know the location of a card with the same rank as the new discard, you may try to throw it.

Suit does not matter. Only rank matters.

### Reacting With Your Own Card

If you correctly discard your own matching card:

- that card leaves your hand
- you now have one fewer card

### Reacting With Another Player's Card

If you correctly discard another player's matching card:

- that target card is discarded
- you must give that player 1 of your own cards
- you end with one fewer card overall

### Wrong Guess Penalty

If you throw the wrong card:

- that attempted card is revealed to all players at its current location
- the card stays where it is
- you draw 1 penalty card face down into your hand
- the reaction window stays open

Each player is limited to 1 wrong reaction attempt per discard event by default.

### Priority

Only one successful reaction can resolve for each discard event.

If multiple claims compete:

- first valid claim applied wins

## Power And Reaction At The Same Time

When a power card is used:

- the card is discarded first
- the power becomes available
- the reaction window for that discarded rank also opens

So power and reaction begin from the same discard event.

If they compete, priority goes to whoever acts first.

## Kaboom

After round 1, on your own turn, you may call Kaboom instead of making any other move.

When you call Kaboom:

- your cards are revealed
- your score is exposed
- your cards become locked
- the other players get one final round

The game ends when turn order comes back to the player who called Kaboom.

During that final round, other players cannot interact with the Kaboom caller's cards.

## End Of Game

The game ends when either:

1. a player reaches zero cards and wins instantly
2. the Kaboom final round finishes

If the game ends by scoring:

- total the points in each player's remaining hand
- the lowest score wins
